What is BS CpE?

  • Objectives
  • Scope
  • Significance
  • Technical Proficiency
    To equip students with a strong foundation in both computer science and electrical engineering, enabling them to design, develop, and maintain computer systems and hardware.
  • Problem-Solving Skills
    To cultivate analytical and problem-solving skills, allowing students to tackle complex engineering challenges in the field of computer systems and hardware.
  • Innovation and Creativity
    To foster creativity and innovation, encouraging students to develop novel solutions, products, and technologies in the computer engineering domain.
  • Interdisciplinary Understanding
    To promote an understanding of the interdisciplinary nature of computer engineering and its applications in various industries.
  • Ethical and Professional Standards
    To instill ethical and professional values, emphasizing the importance of responsible engineering practices, teamwork, and lifelong learning.
  • Hardware Development
    Computer engineering graduates can work on designing and developing computer hardware components such as microprocessors, memory systems, and integrated circuits.
  • Embedded Systems
    They can design embedded systems for various applications, including robotics, automotive, and IoT devices.
  • Software Development
    Graduates may also work on software development, especially in areas closely related to hardware, such as device drivers and firmware.
  • Networking
    Computer engineers can specialize in networking, working on the design and maintenance of computer networks and communication systems.
  • Cybersecurity
    With the increasing importance of cybersecurity, computer engineering graduates are well-equipped to work in this field, ensuring the security of computer systems and networks.
  • Technological Advancement
    Computer engineering plays a crucial role in advancing technology, driving innovations in computing systems, and enabling the development of new products and services.
  • Career Opportunities
    Graduates of this program have a wide range of career opportunities in industries such as electronics, telecommunications, aerospace, automotive, and software development.
  • Problem Solvers
    Computer engineers are problem solvers who bridge the gap between hardware and software, addressing real-world challenges in diverse domains.
  • Critical Infrastructure
    Computer engineers contribute to the development and maintenance of critical infrastructure, including data centers, telecommunications networks, and transportation systems.
  • Economic Growth
    The skills and expertise of computer engineers contribute significantly to economic growth by fostering innovation and competitiveness in the technology sector.
